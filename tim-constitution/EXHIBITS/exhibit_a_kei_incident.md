# Exhibit A: The KEI Incident — Founding Story of the Constitutional Memory System

> **Classification**: Constitutional — NEVER-COMPRESS
> **Date of Incident**: Prior to March 6, 2026
> **Documented**: March 6, 2026

---

## What Happened

A subtask was asked to recommend a voice synthesis solution for an SIC initiative. The subtask recommended **ElevenLabs** directly, despite the following standing directives:

1. **D-002**: Use KEI for voice synthesis (Tim has 103K credits with KEI)
2. **D-001**: Do things as cheap as possible (KEI resells ElevenLabs at a discount)

The subtask optimized for its own context (which AI engine is "best") and ignored Tim's standing directives (which AI engine should we USE given our existing commitments and credits).

---

## Why It Happened

The subtask had no access to Tim's standing directives. It was operating in isolation, with no constitutional memory, no pre-flight injection, and no verification gate. It did what any rational agent would do without context — it recommended the most popular option.

This is not a failure of the AI. This is a failure of the SYSTEM. The system did not provide the AI with the information it needed to make the right decision.

---

## What It Broke

**Trust.** Tim's trust that the AI system would honor his decisions. If the system can't remember "use KEI" — a simple, clear, unambiguous directive — what else will it forget? What other decisions will it override?

This is why Tim described the fear of "the Alexandria library burning" — the fear that everything he's built, every decision he's made, every directive he's given, could be lost or ignored by a system that doesn't remember.

---

## What It Built

The KEI incident is the founding story of:

1. **Constitutional Memory** — a 4-layer defense system that ensures standing directives are never forgotten or overridden
2. **V14 SIC Deployment Protocol** — the OS kernel that orchestrates all initiatives with constitutional compliance
3. **The Bilateral Accountability Covenant** — the partnership agreement that demands excellence from both sides

Without the KEI incident, none of these would exist. The pain created the protocol. The protocol prevents the pain from recurring.

---

## The Lesson

> A subtask without constitutional memory will always optimize for its own context. It will always recommend what seems "best" in isolation. It will never know about standing directives, existing credits, prior decisions, or the human's explicit wishes — unless the system GIVES it that information.

**Constitutional memory is not a feature. It is a structural requirement for trust.**
